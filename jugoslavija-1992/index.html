SWEDEN 1992 · Players II

The Team That Never Played

Sweden · June 1992

A storyline image goes here — drop assets/storyline.jpg

By the moment the mighty Blues had already arrived in Sweden, the tournament they had earned was being taken away from them.

Yugoslavia did not stumble into Euro 92 — they marched in. Through qualification they finished ahead of Denmark, with Darko Pančev top-scoring across the whole of Europe. A golden generation — Stojković, Savićević, Pančev, Prosinečki, Mijatović, Šuker, Bokšić, Mihajlović — was arguably the most gifted side never to kick a ball at a major finals.

As the squad assembled and travelled north, the country behind the shirt was coming apart. On 30 May 1992 the United Nations Security Council passed Resolution 757, placing the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia under sanctions. Ten days before the opening match, UEFA expelled the team. The players, already in Sweden, packed their bags and went home.

Denmark, the side Yugoslavia had pipped in qualifying, were called up as last-minute replacements — and went on to win the entire tournament. The Yugoslavia stickers had already been printed, which is why this album exists at all: a paper memorial to a team that qualified, arrived, and was never allowed to play.

Source stickers: Panini / Decje Novine — UEFA Euro Sweden 1992. Compiled as a personal commemorative album.
